# StreamlineHQ: Getting Started Guide

> **Note:** StreamlineHQ is a fictional product used for demonstration purposes in this example pack.

StreamlineHQ is the internal project management tool built by the engineering team. This guide covers everything you need to start using it effectively in your first week.

## Logging In

Navigate to `app.streamlinehq.internal` and sign in with your company email and onboarding password.

**Forgot your password?** Click **Reset via SSO** to authenticate through Okta. Do not use the standard "forgot password" email flow — it is disabled for company accounts.

## Dashboard Overview

After login you land on the **Dashboard**:

| Area | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| Top bar | Active sprint name, open tasks assigned to you, flagged blockers |
| Left sidebar | My Tasks, Team Board, Projects, Reports |
| Center panel | Your task list for the current sprint |

## Creating a Task

Click the **+** button (top right) or press **C** from anywhere in the app.

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| Title | Required |
| Assignee | Defaults to you |
| Priority | Low / Medium / High / Critical |
| Estimated hours | Used for velocity calculations |
| Labels | Free-form tags: `bug`, `feature`, `tech-debt` |
| Sprint | Defaults to current sprint |

Press **Enter** or click **Save**. The task appears on the Team Board immediately.

## Kanban Board

The **Team Board** has four default columns:

1. **Backlog** — not yet started
2. **In Progress** — actively being worked on
3. **Review** — awaiting code review or QA
4. **Done** — completed this sprint

Drag cards between columns to update status. Changes are reflected in real time for all team members.

## Linking Pull Requests

Connect your GitHub account in **Settings → Integrations**. Then reference a task in your PR description:

```
closes TASK-42
```

When the PR is merged, the linked task automatically moves to the **Review** column.

## Reading Sprint Reports

The **Reports** section displays:

- **Velocity chart** — story points or estimated hours completed per sprint (last 6 sprints)
- **Burndown chart** — daily progress toward sprint completion

If the burndown line is **above** the ideal slope, the sprint is running behind schedule. Use this as an early signal to re-prioritize or flag blockers.
